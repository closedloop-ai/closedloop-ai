ClosedLoop.ai is a bet on one claim about AI-native software delivery: **the spec is the interface**.

In a chat-first workflow, intent is expressed in an ephemeral turn and lost the moment it runs. In a spec-driven workflow, intent is expressed as a durable artifact that can be versioned, reviewed, linked, judged, and replayed. The AI is a participant in the team's specification discipline, not a parallel improvisation channel.

## What "spec-driven" means here

Three commitments:

1. **Intent is written down.** A PRD, a plan, or a ticket captures the problem, the desired outcome, and the acceptance criteria. This is what the AI reads.
2. **Execution is bounded by the spec.** A loop inherits the artifact's scope and can only succeed by satisfying it. The orchestrator emits a `<promise>COMPLETE</promise>` only when the plan's `pendingTasks` are empty and the acceptance criteria validate.
3. **Review is against the spec.** Judges score the plan or the code against the artifact that launched the loop. Human review closes against the same artifact.

## Why this matters for AI-first processes

- **Ambiguity is priced in early.** Under-specified PRDs are caught by the PRD judges (auditor, dependency, scope, testability) before a single line of code is generated. You will not waste a loop on a plan with no measurable success criteria.
- **Work composes.** A plan amends a PRD. A PR closes a plan. A judge report gates a merge. Each step produces a durable artifact that the next step can reference.
- **Autonomy is bounded.** Long-running loops are safe because they execute inside an artifact's scope, under a sandbox, with an approval policy. They are not open-ended agent excursions.
- **Memory accrues.** Self-learning captures patterns, conventions, and mistakes across loops. The team's model of the codebase improves instead of resetting with every new chat.

## How the product operationalizes this

- **Bootstrap plugin** reads your codebase and generates project-specific expert agents plus a `critic-gates.json`. Your specs inherit your code's reality.
- **`/code:code` orchestrator** runs an 18-phase flow that always starts from pre-exploration and the plan, never from a freeform prompt.
- **Judges** run against the artifact's acceptance criteria and engineering principles (DRY, KISS, SSOT, SOLID, convention adherence, codebase grounding).
- **Amendments** happen on the plan artifact (`/code:amend-plan`), not in a new chat. The change is captured, versioned, and attached.
- **Post-loop code review** automatically runs `/code-review:start` against the diff and feeds the verdict into an optional fix cycle.

## The operating model

When a team adopts ClosedLoop.ai it usually evolves into a rhythm like this:

1. A product owner drafts a PRD. PRD judges and teammates iterate until it passes.
2. A plan is generated from the PRD. Plan judges and the team iterate until the plan is review-grade.
3. The plan is executed as one or more loops. Implementation, testing, and visual QA run inside the loop.
4. Post-loop code review produces a verdict. A fix cycle resolves non-approving findings.
5. A PR is opened. Human review closes against the original artifact.
6. Self-learning captures what worked and what did not, so the next loop starts smarter.

That is spec-driven, AI-first delivery, and it is what the rest of this documentation describes in detail.
